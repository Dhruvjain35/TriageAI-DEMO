Why we built this

Every year, emergency departments across the world process over 130 million visits. Each patient is triaged in under five minutes by a nurse who must decide — based on limited information and under immense pressure — how urgently that patient needs care.

Studies show nurses disagree with each other 20–40% of the time. That disagreement costs lives.

We built triage.ai to give every triage nurse an AI second opinion — not to replace their judgment, but to catch the cases they might miss.

From bedside to prediction in five steps

Raw patient data to a calibrated, uncertainty-aware triage recommendation in under 200ms.

01

Data Ingestion

Vitals, demographics, chief complaint text, arrival context, comorbidity history.

12 structured features + free-text
02

Feature Engineering

816 features: clinical composites (qSOFA, SIRS, shock index, 14 vital flags), dual-channel TF-IDF, and Bayesian target encoding.

Fold-aware encoding prevents leakage
03

4-Model Ensemble

LightGBM, XGBoost, CatBoost, and MLP trained with 5-fold CV. Out-of-fold predictions feed the meta-learner.

Diverse inductive biases reduce variance
04

Meta-Learner

L1-regularized logistic regression learns per-class model weights. Dual differential-evolution + Nelder-Mead threshold optimization.

Outperforms naive argmax by 0.5–2%
05

Conformal Prediction

Split conformal at five alpha levels provides prediction sets with distribution-free coverage guarantees.

No distributional assumptions required
